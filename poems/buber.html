poem ending with a line by martin buber

about how a person leaves a hole
when they leaveno room for you to unfold
as you see god you say when they say me
too much comes and splays you out you say
something to a person you alone hear since
you are alone because you are one
in life and in death neither exist or remembering
the planet does its job and then stops
you give away the black bells you plucked
from the house on fire like a mystic nut
falls from your mouth rolls under a carousel
and the whirl of doom congeals
