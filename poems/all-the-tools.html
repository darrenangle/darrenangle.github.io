All the tools are here to take you exactly where you want to go

a small man is hanging over the edge of a railroad track
watching a mouse with a band-aid in its mouth
and laughing a small laugh

a large man with a beard
that appears to be made of mice and band-aids
approaches the smaller man and says

I don't know why you're laughing
that'll be you in thirteen years

he jumps down onto the tracks
collects the mouse into his beard
and bursts into shadow

This poem first appeared in LIT no. 22
